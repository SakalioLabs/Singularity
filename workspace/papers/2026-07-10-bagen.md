# Paper Card: BAGEN

**Title**: BAGEN: Are LLM Agents Budget-Aware?

**Source**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00198

**Date Reviewed**: 2026-07-10

**Core Method**: Treats budget as an active control signal. At every trajectory prefix, an estimator returns a lower/upper interval for remaining cost or declares the task impossible. A rollout-replay protocol separates budget-estimation quality from task-completion quality.

**Evidence**:
- Evaluates five frontier agents across Sokoban, Search-R1, SWE-bench, and a warehouse environment with internal and external budgets.
- Reports systematic optimistic bias and late failure recognition.
- Reports that early stopping saves 28%-64% of failed-trajectory tokens with a 1.6%-4.2% success cost in its evaluated settings.
- Interval coverage remains difficult and reaches about 47% after the reported SFT+RL treatment.

**Useful Transfer**:
- Maintain planner rounds as a live ledger, not only a post-run metric.
- Emit an explicit remaining-round interval and infeasibility alert for every frontier branch.
- Score interval coverage and optimistic misses on held-out prefixes before planner-facing use.
- Keep model-free deterministic estimates visibly uncalibrated until paired live traces qualify them.

**Project Action**: `frontier_budget_interval_v1` provides deterministic per-branch intervals, fixed-budget conservation, and BAGEN-style infeasibility alerts. `frontier-rollout-budget-report` uses independent paired session logs to compute an exact one-sided coverage lower bound and optimistic-miss rate. Only a held-out-qualified report can place the interval in planner context.

**Non-Claim**: Singularity has not trained a BAGEN estimator or reproduced its benchmark results. Current intervals are typed heuristics whose calibration status is explicit.

# Paper Card: Information Gain-based Rollout Policy Optimization

**Title**: Information Gain-based Rollout Policy Optimization: An Adaptive Tree-Structured Rollout Approach for Multi-Turn LLM Agents

**Source**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06223

**Date Reviewed**: 2026-07-10

**Core Method**: Allocates a fixed tree-rollout budget according to node-level informativeness. More informative intermediate states receive more expansions, while low-value branches are progressively suppressed. The induced trajectory distribution becomes an explicit teacher distribution for policy optimization.

**Evidence**:
- Evaluated on seven search-augmented question-answering benchmarks.
- Reports consistent improvements over strong baselines under matched rollout budgets.
- The evidence is for multi-turn search agents, not embodied Minecraft control.

**Useful Transfer**:
- Treat Minecraft task-frontier nodes as competing rollout branches rather than spending equal planner calls on every alternative.
- Estimate information gain from verifier uncertainty reduction, newly satisfied prerequisites, world-model cell discovery, and causal counterexample resolution.
- Reallocate planner rounds saved by a certified episode abort to a fresh or more informative branch, but account for the retry in the same total budget.

**Project Action**: Add an offline frontier-budget report after live M1/M2 traces exist. It should compare uniform allocation with information-weighted branch expansion while holding planner, action backend, total planner-round budget, task stream, and seed fixed. Do not enable online branch suppression until held-out completion and verifier outcomes show no regression.

**Non-Claim**: Singularity has not implemented IGRPO training or reproduced its QA results. The current task-frontier and episode-abort modules only expose future integration points.

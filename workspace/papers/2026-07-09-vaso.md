# Paper Card: VASO

**Title**: VASO: Formally Verifiable Self-Evolving Skills for Physical AI Agents
**Source**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05395
**Date**: 2026
**Type**: Verification-guided self-evolving skill contracts
**Core Problem**: Execution success, rewards, or self-critique show sampled trace quality but do not prove that reusable skills satisfy safety or temporal contracts under untested conditions.
**Core Method**: Represents skills with semantic contracts, checks logical consistency, verifies induced plans against temporal specifications, and turns counterexamples into textual gradients for contract updates.
**Memory / Task Mechanism**:
  - Separates planner-facing skill guidance from formal verification interfaces.
  - Uses counterexample traces as optimization feedback rather than direct trust signals.
  - Requires consistency checks before self-evolution updates are committed.
**Borrowable Points**:
  - Keep self-evolution feedback advisory until a verifier/gate approves durable runtime behavior changes.
  - Treat failed traces as counterexamples that should become reviewable gradients.
  - Attach safety-gate metadata to every planner-facing repair hint.
**Singularity Adaptation**:
  - `SelfEvolutionPolicy` exposes `safety_gate=advisory_only_requires_verification`.
  - Self-evolution feedback can guide planning context, but it cannot bypass goal verification, skill promotion gates, discovery gates, or skill graph governance.
**Next Action**: Add gate reports for any future automatic plan-suffix repair or generated skill-body update.

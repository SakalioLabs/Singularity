# Paper Card: Optimus-2

**Title**: Optimus-2: Multimodal Minecraft Agent with Goal-Observation-Action Conditioned Policy
**Source**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.19902
**Project**: https://cybertronagent.github.io/Optimus-2.github.io/
**Date**: 2025
**Authors**: Zaijing Li, Yuquan Xie, Rui Shao, Gongwei Chen, Dongmei Jiang, Liqiang Nie
**Type**: Paper + Dataset
**Core Problem**: Minecraft agents need to model the causal relation between goals, observations, and actions over time.
**Core Method**: MLLM high-level planning plus Goal-Observation-Action Conditioned Policy for low-level control.
**System Architecture**:
  - Action-guided behavior encoder summarizes observation-action history.
  - Behavior tokens condition action prediction.
  - MGOA dataset provides goal-observation-action pairs from videos.
**Memory Mechanism**: Fixed-length behavior tokens over history rather than explicit symbolic memory.
**Task Mechanism**: Use goal and historical observation-action context to predict low-level actions.
**Key Results**: Reports superior performance across atomic, long-horizon, and open-ended Minecraft tasks.
**Reproduction Difficulty**: High; dataset and policy training are heavy.
**Borrowable Points**:
  - Keep action history compact as behavior tokens or summaries.
  - Treat action selection as conditioned on goal + observation + recent action trace.
  - Use high-level planner plus low-level actor split.
**Current Singularity Mapping**:
  - `MemorySystem` records recent contexts and episodes.
  - `RuntimeSupervisor` begins the high-level/low-level separation.
**Next Action**: Add compact action-history summaries to planner prompts and experience atoms.

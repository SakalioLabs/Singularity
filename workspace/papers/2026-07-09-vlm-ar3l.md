# Paper Card: VLM-AR3L

**Title**: VLM-AR3L: Vision-Language Models for Absolute and Relative Rewards in Reinforcement Learning
**Source**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00483
**Date**: 2026
**Type**: VLM reward learning / embodied progress evaluation
**Core Problem**: Open-ended embodied tasks often lack dense, reliable reward functions, especially when task success is visual, gradual, or easier to compare between states than to score absolutely.
**Core Method**: Uses VLM-generated labels to train absolute rewards for individual states and relative rewards for consecutive observations, then combines both for more stable progress evaluation.
**Memory / Task Mechanism**:
  - Scores single observations for task compatibility.
  - Compares consecutive observations to detect progress or regression.
  - Emphasizes Minecraft as an open-world benchmark with visual complexity and long-horizon decision making.
**Borrowable Points**:
  - Keep absolute state quality and relative progress/regression as separate report fields.
  - Use consecutive observation comparisons to catch stagnation before final goal verification fails.
  - Later replace heuristic progress labels with screenshot/VLM preference labels once real screenshots are available.
